Kottayam, a city in central Kerala, India, is known for its unique blend of natural beauty and cultural significance. It's a land of backwaters, paddy fields, and rubber plantations, earning it the nickname "Akshara Nagari" or "city of letters" due to its contribution to print media and literature. kottayam.nic.in

Key Features:
Natural Beauty: Surrounded by the Western Ghats and Vembanad Lake, Kottayam boasts picturesque backwaters, lush paddy fields, and extensive rubber plantations.
Cultural Significance: It's a major center for rubber production and a hub for spice trade. The city also has a rich literary history and is known for its literate population.
Tourist Attractions: Thirunakkara Mahadeva Temple, Kumaranalloor Devi temple, and various churches and religious sites.
Unique Characteristics: Known for its blend of highlands, hills, and the Kuttanad region's paddy fields.
"Land of Letters, Legends, Latex and Lakes": This reflects Kottayam's literary heritage, legendary history, rubber production, and the Vembanad Lake.
