Pathanamthitta, a district in Kerala, India, is known for its scenic beauty, pilgrimage sites, and cultural heritage. It's often called the "Headquarters of Pilgrimage Tourism" due to the Sabarimala temple and other religious centers. The district also boasts lush forests, hills, rivers, and rural landscapes.

Key Features of Pathanamthitta:

  • Pilgrimage Destination: Famous for the Sabarimala Ayyappan Temple.
  • Natural Beauty: Hilly terrain, forests, and rivers offering picturesque views.
  • Cultural Significance: Rich in traditions, festivals, and historical sites.
