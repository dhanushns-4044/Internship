Kollam, formerly known as Quilon, is a city and district in the southern Indian state of Kerala, located 71 km north of the state capital, Thiruvananthapuram. It's a coastal city known for its cashew processing and coir manufacturing industries. Kollam also serves as the gateway to the backwaters of Kerala and is a prominent tourist destination. Kollam Municipal Corporation Key aspects of Kollam: Location: Southern Kerala, bordering the Arabian Sea, Thiruvananthapuram, Alappuzha, Pathanamthitta, and Tamil Nadu. Administration: District headquarters is at Kollam City. The district is divided into two revenue divisions (Kollam and Punalur) and six taluks. Economy: The cashew industry is a major part of Kollam's economy, with the city being a major producer and exporter of cashews. Coir manufacturing is also significant. Tourism: Kollam is a popular tourist destination, particularly for its backwaters, beaches, and historical sites. Historical Significance: Kollam has a rich history, including its role as a major trading center in ancient times and its connection to the start of the Malayalam era. Natural Beauty: The district is blessed with the Ashtamudi Lake, a large, multi-branched water body, and has a long coastline with pristine beaches. .
